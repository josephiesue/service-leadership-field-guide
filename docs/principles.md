# Principles

## 1. Service Before Status

Leadership begins with contribution, not performance theater.

Status asks:

> How do I appear?

Service asks:

> What does this situation need from me?

## 2. Growth Through Responsibility

Development accelerates when people take responsibility for meaningful outcomes.

Responsibility should be:

- Clear
- Bounded
- Real
- Supported
- Connected to feedback

## 3. Systems Beat Motivation

Motivation matters, but systems decide whether motivation survives contact with reality.

Useful systems include:

- Meeting rhythms
- Reflection prompts
- Recognition routines
- Clear decision rights
- Feedback loops
- Learning rituals

## 4. Clarity Creates Agency

People cannot act well when everything is vague.

Service Leadership values:

- Clear expectations
- Clear next actions
- Clear standards
- Clear ownership
- Clear purpose

## 5. Legacy Is Multiplication

The strongest leaders develop others who can lead without them.

Legacy is not reputation alone.

Legacy is multiplied capacity.

## 6. Recognition Reinforces Culture

What a team notices becomes what the team repeats.

Recognition should not only celebrate exceptional output. It should also make ordinary contribution visible.

## 7. Learning Culture Shapes Engagement

People engage differently depending on the culture around them.

Engagement is not just an individual trait. It is shaped by the relationship between motivation and environment.
